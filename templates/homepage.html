Why do you need Deep Pool AI?


This proposed project introduces a smart system designed to keep swimming pools safer by quickly detecting potential drowning incidents. By using advanced technologies like computer vision and deep learning, the system analyzes video footage from pool cameras in real-time. It starts by spotting objects in the pool and then focuses on understanding people's activities. If there's only one person in the video, the system uses a special learning model to figure out if they are swimming normally or if there's a risk of drowning. For cases where more than one person is seen, the system uses clever logic to decide if there's any unusual situation, like someone being too close to another person. The project ensures that the information is shown immediately on the screen, including any potential drowning situations. Users can also interact with the system by stopping the video whenever they want. An alert system that can quickly inform lifeguards or others if there's a possible drowning is present. This smart system is all about using technology to make swimming pools safer and prevent accidents.

Drowning has a centuries-long history, with records demonstrating its frequency and the ongoing fight to lessen its effects. As communities evolved, so did efforts to adopt basic safety precautions. Despite these advances, drownings continued to haunt society. The onset of the industrial age saw greater urbanization and the proliferation of swimming pools, highlighting the importance of good drowning prevention techniques. The acute health effects of drowning in toddlers are significant and life-threatening. Toddlers, with their limited swimming abilities and immature motor skills, are especially vulnerable to the immediate risk of death associated with drowning. Survivors of such situations may face a variety of secondary health difficulties, including respiratory problems, pneumonia, and neurological consequences. The long-term health consequences are similarly troubling, with prolonged oxygen deprivation potentially leading to permanent cerebral damage. These deficits can have a major influence on cognitive and physical function.

Furthermore, drowning accidents may cause delays in psychomotor development, limiting a toddler's capacity to meet critical developmental milestones. The simultaneous hazard of immediate mortality and long-term health consequences emphasizes the crucial significance of preventative ‎actions and increased monitoring in aquatic environments for child safety.‎ The aftermath of a drowning occurrence can cause long-term trauma for children, impacting their emotional and psychological well-being. Survivors may have a persistent fear of water, which can lead to increased anxiety and reluctance to participate in water-related activities. The stress may extend to ‎the realm of dreams, as children may have nightmares and flashbacks about ‎their horrific drowning experience, further impairing their emotional ‎stability.‎ Simultaneously, the consequences of a drowning incident resonate through the parents, causing significant distress. Parents frequently struggle with excessive emotions of guilt and self-blame, questioning their ability to provide a safe environment for their children. Witnessing or responding to a ‎drowning occurrence can cause long-term mental trauma for parents and ‎carers. ‎ This dual narrative of trauma, which affects both the toddler and their caregivers, emphasises the importance of comprehensive support networks and preventive actions to reduce the psychological burden associated with drowning occurrences in toddlers.
